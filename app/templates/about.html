About Heart Disease Predictor

Dataset

The patients in the dataset used to train the model were contributed by the Hungarian Institute of Cardiology, Zurich University Hospital, Basel University Hospital, US Veteran Affairs Medical Center, and Cleveland Clinic Foundation.

The dataset consisted of 303 anonymized patient records with 13 independent variables and one dependent variable. Below are visualizations of the data from an exploratory data analysis.



Model

The model used to make predictions is a logistic regression model with L2 regularization. It was evaluated on 30 randomly chosen patients and achieved an accuracy of 90% and correctly predicted all 14 cases of heart disease. Below is a plot of the logistic regression model coefficients. A positive value indicates the variable increases the probability of a heart disease diagnosis while a negative value decreases it.
